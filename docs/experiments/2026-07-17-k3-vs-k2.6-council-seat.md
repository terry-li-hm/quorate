# K3 versus K2.6 council-seat capability evaluation

## Decision

Replace Kimi K2.6 with K3 in the existing six-model Quorate council. Use the subscribed Kimi Code CLI route and fail closed if that route is unavailable. This role decision does not alter K3's separate failure of Vivesca's coding-route gate.

## Why a local evaluation was necessary

K3's public agentic, automation, and long-context results establish that it is a serious candidate, but they do not measure marginal contribution under Quorate's concise blind phase. The local capability evaluation therefore changed one variable: the model and route behind the Kimi seat. Twenty fixed, synthetic, non-sensitive decision cases represented architecture, product, risk, evidence, and organizational trade-offs.

K2.6 ran through the incumbent OpenRouter route. K3 ran through Kimi Code 0.26.0 with the subscribed `kimi-code/k3` route. Both received the same question, blind-phase instructions, output allowance, and timeout. Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol judged anonymized, order-balanced response pairs against a hidden case-specific criterion and the considerations already expected from the other five seats.

The locked primary metric awarded K3 one point for a pairwise win, half a point for a tie, and zero for a loss. Replacement required at least 24 of 40 possible points plus non-compensable reachability, concise-output, timeout, and median-latency floors. A weak judge route or fewer than 18 reachable K2.6 responses would invalidate the comparison.

## Result

K3 earned 35.0 of 40 possible points across 38 completed judgments. Fable awarded K3 18 wins and K2.6 one; GPT awarded K3 17 wins and K2.6 two. Neither judge tied a pair, and both completed every judgment presented to them. Two possible judgments were absent because K2.6 returned one empty OpenRouter response; treating both missing judgments as zero K3 points is conservative.

K3 was reachable and output-compliant in all 20 cases. Its mean latency was 28.18 seconds and median was 26.76 seconds. K2.6 was reachable and compliant in 19 cases, with mean latency 41.44 seconds and median 38.97 seconds. K3 therefore cleared every predeclared operational floor as well as the quality threshold.

The aggregate report is stored outside the repository at `~/.local/state/quorate/council-seat-assays/2026-07-17-k3-vs-k2.6.json`. It retains no model response text.

After the roster change, the fixed production canaries were healthy: all nine production and benchmark roles were reachable and passed all three cases. K3 passed 3 of 3 through `kimi-code` at 9.6 seconds mean latency. The aggregate snapshot is `~/.local/state/quorate/benchmarks/2026-07-17.json` and retains no response text.

## Implementation

The fourth council seat now defaults to `kimi-code/k3`. Quorate invokes Kimi Code in an isolated temporary workspace with no discovered skills, strips provider credentials from its subprocess environment, parses only assistant events from the structured stream, and does not send K3 through an API fallback when the subscription route fails. `CONSILIUM_MODEL_M4` can still override the seat for controlled comparisons.
